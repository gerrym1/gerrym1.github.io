Hi, ladies and gentlemen, this is Gerry Moriarty with some current information on the real estate market. As always, we’re going to give you some news and we’re going to give you the updates. The news is pretty positive and I want to make sure that everyone realizes that because there are some mass-media sources that are trying to convey a situation where maybe the economy is not doing as well or that the housing market is struggling right now with affordability. We do have some challenges. The challenges are inventory, but I want to make sure that you know how to counter some of those false narratives that are out there right now. So, let’s jump right into the news. First off, a quote from MarketWatch, “Consumer confidence rose in July to the second highest level in 16 years as Americans shrugged off all the drama in Washington and took heart in the best labor market in a decade.” Again, some of the drama that people are getting distracted with, all right, and media is getting distracted with the American people are not. They understand they’re in a better position as far as their job is concerned. The MarketWatch quote goes on to say, “The Conference Board said its consumer confidence index rose to 121.1 this month from 117.3 in June. The increase exceeded the 116.9 forecast of economists polled by MarketWatch.” So, make sure that you catch what’s being said there. The economists that they polled prior to the number coming out thought that consumer confidence was going to drop and instead it increased to the second highest level in 16 years. So, though there are some people, mostly in the media, that are spinning one narrative what’s actually happening to the American people is they’re feeling confident and they’re out there doing things. And probably the best indicator of that is the second quarter homeownership rate report that came out from the census. For the first time this decade the homeownership rate actually went up. It was almost in a free dive. Some people thought that it was going to hit 50 percent, but as you can see, the second quarter of 2016 last year was at 62.9 and now, it jumped up to 63.7, the highest level in three years. So, things are changing in the economy and things are changing in housing right now where more and more people are jumping, as a percentage, are jumping into home ownership. As a matter of fact, Ivy Zelman in her latest Z Report said this, “On a seasonally adjusted basis, the homeownership rate averaged 63.8 in the first half of 2017. If that holds through the remainder of the year, which we believe is likely 2017 will stand higher than both 2016 and 2015.” So, what Ivy Zelman is projecting is that the homeownership rate at the end of this year is going to be higher than it’s been in three years. So, again ladies and gentlemen, people are jumping back in as far as purchasing a home is concerned and David H. Stevens, the Mortgage Bankers Association President and CEO said this, “The bottom line is that the economy is strong and improving. Interest rates remain low and employment is high, which when combined with the coming demographic shift as more millennials enter prime household-creating years, suggest the housing market is on a steady course for the foreseeable future.” That's why there are so many buyers in the market. That’s why demand is so high.
